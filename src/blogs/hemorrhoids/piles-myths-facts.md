## Myths and Facts about Hemorrhoids in Nepal!

## First: What exactly are hemorrhoids (piles)?

Hemorrhoids are **swollen veins** around the anus or inside the lower rectumтАФsimilar to varicose veins, but in the back passage.

There are two main types:
- **Internal hemorrhoids:** inside the anus (often painless bleeding)
- **External hemorrhoids:** outside the anus (often painful if thrombosis develops)

**Important:** Piles are common and treatable. The goal is to stop symptoms, prevent recurrence, and rule out serious causes of bleeding.

## Why are piles so common in Nepal?

A few everyday тАЬNepal-lifeтАЭ reasons:
- **Constipation** (low fiber, low water, irregular meals)
- **Long toilet sitting** (phone scrolling = extra pressure)
- **Straining during bowel movement**
- **Spicy/oily diet + low vegetables**
- **Pregnancy and childbirth**
- **Heavy lifting** and long hours of standing/sitting
- **Ignoring early symptoms** until it becomes severe

## Myth vs Fact (NepalтАЩs most common piles beliefs)

### Myth 1: тАЬPiles is always because of spicy food.тАЭ
**Fact:** Spicy food can **irritate symptoms** (burning, discomfort), but piles mainly happen due to **constipation, straining, and pressure**. Many people who never eat spicy food still get piles.

### Myth 2: тАЬIf there is bleeding, it is definitely piles.тАЭ
**Fact:** Bright red blood can be pilesтАФbut bleeding can also come from **anal fissure**, **polyps**, **infection**, **inflammation**, or other bowel conditions.  
тЬЕ **Rule:** If you have bleeding, get a proper examination. DonтАЩt self-diagnose.

### Myth 3: тАЬPiles always needs surgery.тАЭ
**Fact:** Many patients with grade I improve with:
- diet + water + stool softeners
- lifestyle change

Surgery is usually for **grade II, III, and IV piles**. **Laser Treatment** is recent and minimal invasive day care procedure.

### Myth 4: тАЬPiles operation is extremely dangerous and life-long painful.тАЭ
**Fact:** Modern management has improved a lot. Options like **laser therapy (treatment)** can reduce pain and downtime for most of the patients. Recovery depends on the grade, and care after treatment.

### Myth 5: тАЬPiles will turn into cancer.тАЭ
**Fact:** Hemorrhoids **do not turn into cancer**.  
But hereтАЩs the twist: **cancer patient may have piles**. ThatтАЩs why evaluation is importantтАФespecially if you are **over 40**, have **weight loss**, **persistent diarrhea/constipation changes**, or **family history of cancer**.

### Myth 6: тАЬIf it doesnтАЩt hurt, itтАЩs not serious.тАЭ
**Fact:** Internal piles often cause **painless bleeding**. Painless does not mean harmless. Ongoing bleeding can cause **anemia (рдХрдордЬреЛрд░реА/рдЪрдХреНрдХрд░)**.

### Myth 7: тАЬSitting on cold floor causes piles.тАЭ
**Fact:** Cold surfaces donтАЩt directly cause piles. The real causes are **pressure, straining, constipation**, and sometimes pregnancy.

### Myth 8: тАЬHome remedies can cure all piles permanently.тАЭ
**Fact:** Some home steps help symptoms (warm sitz bath, fiber, water). But if piles are advanced, prolapsing, or bleeding repeatedly, home remedies wonтАЩt тАЬreverseтАЭ it fully. They may only delay proper treatment.

## Common symptoms: what patients in Nepal usually report

You may have piles if you notice:
- **Bright red bleeding** during/after motion
- **Itching or irritation**
- **Pain** (more common with external piles or clot)
- **Swelling/lump** near anus
- **Mucus discharge** or feeling of incomplete cleaning
- Tissue (swelling) coming out during motion that goes back in (prolapse)

## Warning signs: when you should see a doctor urgently

Seek medical help soon if you have:
- Heavy bleeding, clots, dizziness, fainting
- Black stool (dark, tar-like) or mixed blood in stool
- Persistent pain with fever
- Sudden very painful swelling (possible thrombosis)
- Unexplained weight loss, loss of appetite
- New bowel habit changes (constipation/diarrhea for weeks)
- Age **>40** with bleeding (needs evaluation)
- Family history of colorectal cancer

## How piles are diagnosed (simple and respectful)

A proper check may include:
- **History + visual examination**
- **Digital rectal exam** (quick, gentle)
- **Proctoscopy/anoscopy** to see internal piles
- In some cases: **sigmoidoscopy/colonoscopy** to rule out other causes of bleeding

Good clinics keep privacy and dignity as top priorityтАФno shame, no drama.
