Hemorrhoids (also known as piles) are a very common problem in Nepal. Swelling near anal area, bleeding during passing stool, itching near anal area, and discomfort while sitting can seriously affect daily activity. Laser treatment for hemorrhoids is a modern, minimally invasive procedure that allows faster recovery, less pain, and early return to work and normal life.

## What Is Laser Treatment for Hemorrhoids?

Laser treatment for hemorrhoids is a modern, minimally invasive approach to treat hemorrhoids. The procedure typically takes 30-40 minutes and can be performed on an outpatient basis, meaning you can return home the same day.

Unlike traditional open surgical methods, laser treatment uses focused light guided diode laser directly into the hemorrhoids (dilated veins) to shrink hemorrhoid tissue with precision and less damage to normal tissue. Compared to traditional open surgery, this advanced technique (laser treatment) offers significant advantages including no large cuts, less pain, faster recovery, early return to work, and less bleeding.

## Assessment of Patient Before Surgery

**Before surgery, your doctor will:**
- Take detailed medical history
- Examine the anal area
- Check grade and type of hemorrhoids
- Review medications (BP, diabetes, blood thinners)
- Order basic blood tests, chest X-ray, ECG if needed

**тЪая╕П Always tell your doctor if you:**
- Have heart, lung, or kidney problems
- Are pregnant
- Are taking aspirin or blood thinners

**Things To Do Before Surgery**
- Do not eat or drink for 6тАУ8 hours before surgery
- Take only medicines advised by your doctor
- Wear loose, comfortable clothes
- Bring a family member
- Arrive on time at the hospital

## Surgery Day

### Preparation and Procedure
- You will be prepared for surgery in the preoperative preparation area
- You will be shifted into operation room
- Anesthetist doctor will perform pre-anesthesia Check-up
- Either laryngeal mask anesthesia or saddle block anesthesia (explained below) will be given to you.
- Positioning, cleaning of surgical field will be done after anesthesia
- Thorough examination under anesthesia will be done by surgeon
- Hemorrhoidal artery ligation using absorbable suture will be done
- A laser probe will be inserted and laser energy will be delivered precisely into the hemorrhoid mass
- Ice-cube will be used for cooling of hemorrhoid mass
- Laser energy shrinks the piles mass from inside
- Procedure usually takes 30тАУ40 minutes after anesthesia

### Observation period after surgery (6-7 hours):
- You will be kept under observation for 6тАУ7 hours
- Blood pressure, pain, and urine output will be monitored
- Once stable, you can go home the same day

### Medicines Prescribed After Surgery

**Your doctor will prescribe:**
- Medicine to relief pain like paracetamole, ketoloroc tablet with PPI
- Stool softeners like syrup lactulose
- Antibiotics (if needed)
- Sometimes local ointments

> тЪая╕П Do not stop or change medicines without consulting your doctor. Follow instruction given by Doctor

### Wound Care After Surgery
- Maintain good anal hygiene
- Warm sitz bath 2-3 times daily and repeat after passage of stool
- Apply ointment in anal area if it is prescribed
- Do not apply home remedies without advice
- Keep area clean and dry

### Wound Care Tips

**Keep the Area Clean:**

-   Shower or bathe daily
-   Gently clean with warm water
-   Avoid using harsh chemicals or antiseptics
-   Ensure complete drying after washing

**Monitor Healing:**

-   Check for signs of infection daily
-   Watch for unusual discharge or odor
-   Note any changes in pain or swelling
-   Keep follow-up appointments with your doctor

**Clothing Choices:**

-   Wear loose, breathable cotton underwear
-   Avoid tight pants or belts
-   Choose comfortable, non-restrictive clothing
-   Change underwear daily

## Anesthesia Used During Laser Hemorrhoid Surgery
**1. LMA (Laryngeal Mask Anesthesia)**
*This is a light general anesthesia*
- You sleep lightly during surgery
- No pain or awareness

*Risk of LMA is very rare and includes:*
- Mild Nausea
- Mild sore throat
- Dizziness after surgery

**2. Saddle Block (Spinal Anesthesia)**
*Fine needle will be used to deliver anesthesia in your back*
- Lower body becomes numb
- You remain awake

*Risk of saddle block is very rate which includes:*
- Temporary back discomfort
- Post-spinal headache (you will feel headache, dizziiness on standing)
- Short-term difficulty passing urine

> Your anesthesiologist will choose the safest option for you.

## Step by Step Recovery Time-line

### Day 1: Immediate Post-Treatment (First 24 Hours)

**What to Expect:**

-   Mild to moderate discomfort in the anal area
-   Slight bleeding or spotting when using the bathroom
-   Feeling of pressure or fullness in the rectum
-   Possible numbness wearing off if local anesthesia was used

**What to Do:**

-   Rest at home and avoid strenuous activities
-   Continue taking sitz bath and cleaning of anal area
-   You can apply ice packs to the area for 15-20 minutes (optional)
-   Take prescribed pain medications as directed
-   Drink plenty of water (8-10 glasses)
-   Eat light, easily digestible foods
-   Avoid sitting for prolonged periods

### Days 2-3: Early Recovery Phase

**What to Expect:**

-   Discomfort may increases during this period
-   Mild swelling around the treated area
-   Some difficulty or hesitation with bowel movements
-   Improved mobility compared to day 1

**What to Do:**

-   Continue pain management as prescribed
-   Start gentle walking around your home
-   Use stool softeners to prevent straining
-   You may increase the dose of stool softener medicine to achieve soft stool and decrease dose to avoid diarrhoea.
-   Take warm sitz baths at least 2-3 times daily (20-25 minutes each) and repeat after passing stool thereafter
-   Apply ointments or creams (if prescribed) after sitz baths and cleaning of anal area
-   Maintain high fluid intake and fiber diet.

### Days 4-7: First Week Progress

**What to Expect:**

-   Noticeable reduction in pain and discomfort
-   Decreased swelling
-   Easier bowel movements
-   Return of normal appetite
-   Minimal to no bleeding

**What to Do:**

-   Gradually increase light activities
-   Continue sitz baths twice daily
-   Maintain soft diet with high fiber
-   Resume short walks outdoors
-   Avoid lifting anything over 5 kg
-   Keep the area clean and dry

### Week 2-4: Progressive Healing

#### Week 2

**What to Expect:**

-   Significant improvement in comfort level
-   Most acute symptoms resolved
-   Normal bowel function returning
-   Increased energy levels

**Activity Guidelines:**

-   Resume most daily activities with caution
-   Walking is encouraged
-   Return to desk work if applicable
-   Avoid heavy lifting, intense exercise, and cycling
-   Sexual activity should wait until cleared by your doctor

#### Weeks 3-4

**What to Expect:**

-   Near-complete healing for most patients
-   Minimal to no pain during bowel movements
-   Tissue regeneration well underway
-   Return to normal routine possible

**Activity Guidelines:**

-   Resume most normal activities
-   Can typically return to gym with modifications
-   Avoid activities that put pressure on the anal area
-   Continue high-fiber diet and adequate hydration

## Follow-Up Care and Medical Appointments

### Scheduled Check-Ups

**First Follow-Up (Week 1-2):**

-   Assessment of initial healing
-   Check for complications
-   Medication review and adjustment
-   Discussion of any concerns or symptoms
-   Guidance on activity progression

**Second Follow-Up (Week 4):**

-   Evaluation of complete healing
-   Clearance for normal activities
-   Long-term prevention strategies
-   Addressing any persistent issues

**Long-Term Monitoring:**

-   Annual check-ups recommended
-   Report any recurrence of symptoms immediately
-   Maintain open communication with your doctor
-   Keep records of bowel habits and any issues

## Warning Signs

### Normal vs. Concerning Symptoms

**Normal Recovery Symptoms:**

-   Mild pain and discomfort for 1-2 weeks
-   Small amount of bleeding with bowel movements initially
-   Temporary swelling around the treated area
-   Feeling tired or low energy in the first week
-   Minor itching as the area heals

**Concerning Symptoms Requiring Medical Attention:**

-   Significant pain despite oral medications for pain relief
-   Fever
-   Pus or foul-smelling discharge from the treated area
-   Increasing swelling or redness
-   Signs of infection (warmth, tenderness, red streaks)

> **тЪая╕П If you develops concerning symptoms, consider OPD visit to see your surgeon.**
If you feel significant difficulties, do not hesitate to visit emergency room of the hospital.

### Emergency Situations

**Seek Immediate Medical Care if You Experience:**

-   Heavy bleeding that doesn't stop or soaks through pads
-   Inability to urinate or pass stool
-   Difficulty breathing or chest pain
-   Severe abdominal pain
-   Loss of consciousness or severe dizziness
-   Allergic reaction (rash, swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing)

## Diet and Nutrition During Recovery

### First Week Diet

**Foods to Eat:**

-   High-fiber fruits: papaya, bananas, apples (with skin)
-   Cooked vegetables: spinach, carrots, pumpkin
-   Whole grains: oats, brown rice, whole wheat bread
-   Lentils and beans (well-cooked)
-   Plenty of water and clear fluids
-   Yogurt and probiotics

**Foods to Avoid:**

-   Spicy foods and hot chilies
-   Processed and junk food
-   Red meat and fried foods
-   Alcohol and caffeinated beverages
-   White bread and refined grains
-   Cheese and dairy products in excess
-   Better to avoid meat and any other food which causes constipation

### Long-Term Dietary Guidelines

**Fiber Intake:**

-   Aim for 25-35 grams of fiber daily
-   Increase fiber gradually to avoid gas and bloating
-   Choose both soluble and insoluble fiber sources
-   Consider fiber supplements if needed (consult doctor)

**Hydration:**

-   Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water (2.5 to 3 lit) daily
-   Include coconut water, fresh fruit juices
-   Limit caffeine and alcohol
-   Proper hydration keeps stools soft

## Physical Activity Timeline After Laser Treatment
- Walking
- Rest on the day of surgery
- Light walking encouraged from next day
- Light Household Work: after 2тАУ3 days
- Desk Job / Office Work: after 7 days
- Sexual Activity: Usually after 2-3 weeks (if pain-free)
- Bike Riding / Cycling: After 2тАУ3 weeks (short travel)
- Long Travel by Road: after 3 weeks
- Gym / Heavy Work: after 8 to 12 weeks

## Preventing Hemorrhoid Recurrence

### Long-Term Lifestyle Changes

**Maintain Regular Bowel Habits:**

-   Don't delay when you feel the urge to defecate
-   Avoid straining during bowel movements
-   Limit time spent on the toilet (no more than 5 minutes)
-   Don't read or use phone while on the toilet

**Exercise Regularly:**

-   Aim for 30 minutes of moderate activity daily
-   Walking, swimming, and yoga are excellent choices
-   Regular exercise improves circulation and prevents constipation
-   Strengthens pelvic floor muscles

**Manage Your Weight:**

-   Maintain a healthy body weight
-   Excess weight increases pressure on pelvic veins
-   Gradual weight loss if overweight
-   Combine diet with regular exercise

**Avoid Risk Factors:**

-   Don't sit for prolonged periods (take breaks every hour)
-   Avoid heavy lifting when possible
-   Don't strain during bowel movements
-   Manage chronic cough or constipation
-   Avoid prolonged standing

### Dietary Habits for Prevention

**High-Fiber Diet:**

-   Consume 25-35 grams of fiber daily
-   Include vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and legumes
-   Add fiber gradually to prevent gas
-   Consider psyllium husk or fiber supplements

**Adequate Hydration:**

-   Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily
-   Increase fluid intake in hot weather
-   Limit dehydrating beverages (coffee, alcohol)
-   Monitor urine color (should be light yellow)

**Foods That Help:**

-   Leafy greens and cruciferous vegetables
-   Berries and citrus fruits
-   Nuts and seeds
-   Whole grain products
-   Fermented foods for gut health

## Hygiene and Wound Care

### Daily Cleaning Routine

**Proper Hygiene Practices:**

-   Clean the anal area gently after each bowel movement
-   Use soft, unscented toilet paper or moist wipes
-   Pat dry instead of rubbing
-   Consider using a bidet or hand-held spray
-   Wash with mild, fragrance-free soap and water

**What to Avoid:**

-   Harsh soaps and scented products
-   Rough toilet paper
-   Excessive wiping or scrubbing
-   Tight-fitting underwear
-   Synthetic fabrics (choose cotton instead)
