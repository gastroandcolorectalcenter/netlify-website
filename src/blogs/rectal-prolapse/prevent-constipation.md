## Diet and Lifestyle to Prevent Constipation

Constipation is a **very common digestive problem** affecting people of all ages. It can cause discomfort, bloating, straining during bowel movements, and can worsen conditions like **hemorrhoids, rectal prolapse, and fissures**.

The good news is that **most cases of constipation can be prevented naturally** with proper diet and healthy daily habits.

This guide explains:
- Why constipation happens  
- What to eat and what to avoid  
- Lifestyle habits that improve bowel movement  
- Practical tips for long-term constipation prevention  

---

## What Is Constipation?

Constipation usually means:
- Passing hard or dry stools  
- Straining during bowel movements  
- Having bowel movements fewer than 3 times a week  
- Feeling of incomplete bowel emptying

## Why Preventing Constipation Is Important

Long-term constipation can lead to:
- Piles (hemorrhoids)
- Anal fissures
- Rectal prolapse
- Worsening bowel control
- Dependence on laxatives

Preventing constipation helps maintain **healthy digestion and pelvic floor strength**.

---

## Diet to Prevent Constipation

### 1. Increase Fiber Intake (Most Important)

Fiber adds bulk to stool and helps it move smoothly through the intestines.

#### Best high-fiber foods:
- Fruits: papaya, apple, pear, orange
- Vegetables: spinach, broccoli, carrot
- Whole grains: oats, brown rice, whole wheat roti
- Legumes: lentils, beans, chickpeas
- Seeds: flaxseed, chia seed

ЁЯСЙ Aim for **25тАУ35 grams of fiber daily**.

---

### 2. Drink Enough Water

Without enough water, fiber can actually worsen constipation.

- Drink **2тАУ3 liters of water daily**
- Increase intake in hot weather or physical activity
- Warm water in the morning can stimulate bowel movement

---

### 3. Foods to Limit or Avoid

Some foods slow digestion and harden stools.

тЭМ Reduce:
- Fast food and junk food
- Fried and oily food
- Excess dairy (cheese, cream)
- Excess tea, coffee, alcohol
- Processed foods

## Lifestyle Habits to Prevent Constipation

### 1. Do Not Ignore the Urge to Pass Stool

- Go to the toilet when you feel the urge
- Repeatedly ignoring urges makes stool harder

---

### 2. Establish a Fixed Toilet Routine

- Best time: **morning after breakfast**
- Sit calmly without straining
- Do not rush or force bowel movement

---

### 3. Correct Toilet Posture

- Raise feet using a small stool
- Lean slightly forward
- This position straightens the rectum and reduces straining

---

### 4. Stay Physically Active

Regular movement stimulates bowel activity.

тЬФ Recommended:
- Walking 30 minutes daily
- Light yoga or stretching
- Avoid prolonged sitting

---

### 5. Avoid Straining During Bowel Movements

Straining increases pressure on:
- Rectum
- Pelvic floor muscles
- Anal veins

This can worsen piles and rectal prolapse.

---

## Constipation Prevention After Surgery

After abdominal or rectal surgery:
- Follow a high-fiber diet gradually
- Use stool softeners if prescribed
- Avoid straining completely
- Drink plenty of fluids

Preventing constipation is **critical for surgical success**.

---

## Should You Use Laxatives Regularly?

- Occasional use may be helpful
- Daily or long-term use is **not recommended**
- Overuse can make the bowel lazy

Always consult a doctor before regular laxative use.

---

## When Should You See a Doctor?

Seek medical advice if:
- Constipation lasts more than 3 weeks
- There is blood in stool
- Severe abdominal pain occurs
- Sudden constipation in older age
- Unexplained weight loss
