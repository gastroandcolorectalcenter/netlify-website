## Why Post-Surgery Care Is So Important

Rectal prolapse occurs due to **weak pelvic floor muscles and chronic straining**.  
Surgery corrects the anatomy, but **poor habits can bring the prolapse back**.

### ЁЯЯв Phase 1: Immediate Recovery (Day 1тАУ7)
- Mild pain and discomfort are normal
- Start walking within 24 hours
- Soft or liquid diet initially
- Avoid straining during bowel movements
- Take prescribed stool softeners

### ЁЯЯб Phase 2: Early Healing (Week 2тАУ4)

## Preventing Constipation: The Golden Rule

Constipation is the **number one cause of recurrence**.

### Tips:
- Never ignore urge to pass stool
- Do not strain on the toilet
- Use a footstool to improve posture
- Limit toilet time to under 5 minutes
- Use stool softeners if advised

---

## Pelvic Floor (Kegel) Exercises After Surgery

Pelvic floor exercises **strengthen the support system** of the rectum.

### How to Do Kegel Exercises:
1. Tighten muscles as if stopping urine
2. Hold for 5 seconds
3. Relax for 5 seconds
4. Repeat 10тАУ15 times, twice daily

## Lifestyle Changes to Prevent Recurrence

- Avoid heavy lifting for at least 3 months
- Maintain healthy body weight
- Treat chronic cough early
- Manage prostate problems in men
- Avoid prolonged sitting on toilet
- Stay physically active (walking, yoga)

---

## Warning Signs You Should Not Ignore

Contact your surgeon if you notice:
- New bulge from anus
- Difficulty controlling stool
- Persistent constipation
- Bleeding or mucus discharge
- Severe pain or fever

Early intervention prevents major complications.

- Permanent relief from prolapse

- Better quality of life

Success rate is **very high** when post-surgery advice is followed properly.
