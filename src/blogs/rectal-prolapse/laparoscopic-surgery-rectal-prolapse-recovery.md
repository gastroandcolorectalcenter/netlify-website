## Laparoscopic Rectal Prolapse Surgery: Advantages, Recovery and Results

Laparoscopic rectal prolapse surgery is a **modern and minimally invasive procedure** designed to permanently correct rectal prolapse. Compared to traditional open surgery, it offers **less pain, faster recovery, and excellent long-term results**.

This article explains the procedure in detailтАФwho needs it, how it is done, its advantages, possible risks, recovery timeline, expected results, diet, and lifestyle care after surgery.

---

## What Is Laparoscopic Rectal Prolapse Surgery?

Laparoscopic rectal prolapse surgery involves repairing rectal prolapse using **small keyhole incisions** in the abdomen. A camera (laparoscope) allows the surgeon to clearly visualize internal structures and correct the prolapse with precision.

The most commonly performed procedure is **laparoscopic rectopexy**, where the rectum is lifted back to its normal position and fixed securely to prevent it from slipping down again.

## Why Choose the Laparoscopic Approach?

### Advantages of Laparoscopic Rectal Prolapse Surgery

Compared to open surgery, laparoscopy offers several important benefits:

- Very small incisions with minimal scarring  
- Less postoperative pain  
- Lower risk of wound infection  
- Reduced blood loss  
- Shorter hospital stay  
- Faster return to daily activities  
- Better visualization for precise repair  
- Lower recurrence rate in suitable patients

## Who Is an Ideal Candidate for Laparoscopic Surgery?

This approach is commonly recommended for:
- Young or middle-aged patients
- Patients medically fit for general anesthesia
- Full-thickness rectal prolapse
- Recurrent prolapse after previous treatment
- Patients seeking long-term durable results

Final decision depends on **age, general health, bowel function, and surgeon expertise**.

---

## Preoperative Preparation (Before Surgery)

Good preparation helps ensure safety and smooth recovery.

### Before surgery, you may be advised to:
- Undergo blood tests, ECG, and imaging studies
- Review current medications with your doctor
- Stop smoking and alcohol
- Temporarily stop blood-thinning drugs (if applicable)
- Follow bowel preparation instructions
- Fast for 6 to 8 hours before surgery

## Risks and Possible Complications

Laparoscopic rectal prolapse surgery is generally safe, but no surgery is completely risk-free.

### Possible risks include:
- Bleeding or infection (uncommon)
- Injury to nearby organs (rare)
- Temporary constipation or bowel changes
- Urinary difficulty (usually short-term)
- Recurrence of prolapse (low risk)
- Anesthesia-related complications

Choosing an **experienced laparoscopic surgeon** significantly minimizes these risks.

## Diet After Laparoscopic Rectal Prolapse Surgery

Diet plays a crucial role in healing and preventing recurrence.

### Recommended diet:
- High-fiber foods (vegetables, fruits, whole grains)
- Plenty of fluids (2тАУ3 liters daily)
- Soft, easily digestible meals initially
- Avoid constipation-causing foods
- Use stool softeners if advised

---

## Lifestyle Changes to Prevent Recurrence

- Avoid straining during bowel movements
- Maintain healthy body weight
- Do pelvic floor exercises (kegel) regularly
- Treat chronic constipation or cough early
- Avoid heavy lifting for at least 3 months

These habits help maintain **long-term surgical success**.
