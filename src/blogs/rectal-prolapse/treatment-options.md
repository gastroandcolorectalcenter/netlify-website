## Rectal Prolapse Treatment Options: Is Surgery Always Needed?

Rectal prolapse is a condition where part or all of the rectum slips out through the anus. It can be distressing, uncomfortable, and disruptive to daily life. One of the most common questions patients ask is: **тАЬCan rectal prolapse be treated without surgery?тАЭ**

The answer depends on **severity, symptoms, age, and overall health**. This guide explains all available treatment options in a clear, patient-friendly way.

---

## What Is Rectal Prolapse?

Rectal prolapse occurs when the supporting muscles and ligaments of the rectum weaken, allowing it to protrude outside the anus, especially during bowel movements.

<div class="flash-card" style="background:#e3f2fd;padding:18px;border-left:4px solid #2196f3;margin:20px 0;">
<strong>ЁЯФН Key Point</strong><br>
Rectal prolapse is a structural problem. While symptoms can be managed, the underlying weakness usually does not fully reverse on its own.
</div>

---

## Treatment Options for Rectal Prolapse

### 1. Non-Surgical (Conservative) Treatment  
**Best for:** mild prolapse, early stages, or patients unfit for surgery  

Non-surgical treatment does **not cure** rectal prolapse but may help control symptoms temporarily.

**Common approaches include:**
- High-fiber diet to prevent constipation
- Stool softeners
- Pelvic floor (Kegel) exercises
- Avoiding straining during bowel movements
- Treating chronic cough or constipation

<div class="flash-card" style="background:#fff8e1;padding:18px;border-left:4px solid #ff9800;margin:20px 0;">
<strong>тЪая╕П Important</strong><br>
Conservative treatment can reduce discomfort, but it rarely fixes the prolapse permanently.
</div>

---

### 2. Surgical Treatment (Definitive Treatment)

**Surgery is the only long-term solution** for most patients with rectal prolapse, especially if:
- The prolapse is full-thickness
- Symptoms are worsening
- There is bleeding, incontinence, or ulcers
- The prolapse occurs frequently or stays outside

---

## Types of Rectal Prolapse Surgery

### A. Abdominal Procedures  
Usually recommended for **younger, healthier patients**.

- Laparoscopic rectopexy (minimally invasive)
- Open rectopexy

**Advantages:**
- Lower recurrence rate
- Better long-term results
- Less pain, minimal scar and faster recovery with laparoscopy

---

### B. Perineal Procedures  
Often chosen for **elderly or high-risk patients**.

- Delorme procedure
- Altemeier procedure

**Advantages:**
- Less stress on the body
- Can be done under regional anesthesia

## Is Surgery Always Needed?

**Not always immediately**, but in most cases **eventually, yes**.

| Condition                 | Surgery Needed?   |
|---------------------------|-------------------|
|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Mild, internal prolapse   | Not immediately   |
| Elderly with high risk    | Sometimes delayed |
| Full-thickness prolapse   | Usually yes       |
| Recurrent prolapse        | Yes               |
|---------------------------|-------------------|

Delaying surgery for too long may lead to worsening symptoms and complications.

---

## Recovery After Rectal Prolapse Surgery

- Hospital stay: 2тАУ5 days (laparoscopic often shorter)
- Return to daily activities: 2тАУ4 weeks
- Avoid heavy lifting: 3 months
- Bowel habits improve gradually

Most patients experience **significant improvement in quality of life** after surgery.
