**How painful is recovery?**\
**When can I return to work or exercise?**\
**Is recovery the same for all hernias?**

If you're planning **laparoscopic hernia surgery**, knowing what recovery looks like can reduce fear and help you heal better. Recovery **depends on the type of hernia and the technique used**, so this guide explains everything clearly---before surgery, right after surgery, and week by week.

I'm **Dr. Surendra Shah**, and this article is written to **prepare you mentally and physically before surgery**.

## Before Surgery: How to Prepare

Good recovery starts **before** the operation.

**A few days before surgery:**

-   Stop smoking (very important for healing)

-   Control diabetes and blood pressure

-   Avoid heavy lifting and straining

-   Inform your surgeon about all medicines and supplements

**Night before surgery:**

-   Do not eat or drink after the advised time (usually midnight)

-   Take only medicines your doctor allows

-   Arrange someone to accompany you home

## In Short: About the Surgery & Anesthesia

-   Surgery is done under **general anesthesia** (you'll be fully asleep)

-   Laparoscopic surgery uses **small cuts** and a camera

-   **Groin hernia surgeries:**\
    TAPP, TEP, or eTEP techniques

-   **Ventral hernia surgeries:**\
    IPOM, IPOM Plus, or eTEP Rives--Stoppa techniques

-   A **mesh** is usually placed to strengthen the weak area

## Immediately After Surgery (Recovery Room)

After surgery:

-   You'll wake up in the recovery room

-   Mild pain, heaviness, or bloating is normal

-   Pain medicines are given

-   You may feel sleepy or slightly nauseous (temporary)

-   Walking usually starts the **same day**

## First 24 Hours in Hospital
--------------------------

**Most laparoscopic hernia patients:**

-   Go home **the same day or after 1 night**

-   Start drinking fluids within a few hours

-   Eat light food once fully awake

-   Walk to the bathroom with support

Pain is usually **mild to moderate** and well controlled with medicines.

## Recovery Depends on Hernia Type
===============================

### ЁЯЯв Groin Hernia Recovery

*(Inguinal & Femoral -- TAPP / TEP / eTEP)*

Groin hernia recovery is usually **faster and easier**.

#### What You May Feel

-   Mild pain or pulling sensation in the groin

-   Mild scrotal swelling or bruising (in men)

-   Shoulder pain from gas (temporary)

#### Week-by-Week Recovery (Groin Hernia)

**Week 1**

-   Walking encouraged

-   Mild pain while moving or coughing

-   Desk work possible in 5--7 days

-   No heavy lifting

**Week 2**

-   Pain reduces significantly

-   Light household work allowed

-   Driving usually safe if pain-free

**Week 3--4**

-   Most daily activities feel normal

-   Light exercise (walking, cycling) allowed

**After 4--6 weeks**

-   Gradual return to gym, sports, and heavy work

-   Core exercises only after clearance

* * * * *

### ЁЯЯа Ventral Hernia Recovery

*(Umbilical, Incisional, Epigastric -- IPOM / IPOM Plus / eTEP Rives-Stoppa)*

Ventral hernia recovery takes **longer** because the repair area is larger.

#### What You May Feel

-   Tightness or heaviness in abdomen

-   More discomfort than groin hernia

-   Abdominal binder often advised

#### Week-by-Week Recovery (Ventral Hernia)

**Week 1**

-   Walking encouraged

-   Binder support used

-   Pain controlled with medicines

-   No bending or lifting

**Week 2--3**

-   Pain slowly reduces

-   Light activities allowed

-   Desk work may resume (case-dependent)

**Week 4--6**

-   Increased comfort

-   Longer walks allowed

-   Still avoid lifting >5--7 kg

**After 6--8 weeks**

-   Gradual return to exercise

-   Core strengthening only after surgeon approval

## When Can I Return To...?

**Work**

-   Desk job:\
    Groin hernia тЖТ 5--10 days\
    Ventral hernia тЖТ 2--4 weeks

-   Heavy physical work: 4--8 weeks (case-dependent)

**Exercise**

-   Walking: immediately

-   Light exercise: 2--3 weeks

-   Gym/heavy exercise: after 4--8 weeks (doctor approval)

**Sex**

-   Usually safe after **2--3 weeks** if pain-free

## Wound Care at Home

-   Keep wounds clean and dry

-   Shower usually allowed after 24--48 hours

-   Do not apply powders or creams unless advised

-   Mild swelling or bruising is normal

-   Watch for signs of infection:

    -   Redness

    -   Fever

    -   Pus

    -   Increasing pain

## Diet After Surgery

-   Start with light, soft food

-   Drink plenty of fluids

-   Eat **high-fiber foods** to avoid constipation

-   Avoid straining during bowel movements

## How to Prevent Hernia Recurrence

Recovery doesn't end when pain ends.

**Important habits:**

-   Avoid heavy lifting early

-   Maintain healthy weight

-   Stop smoking

-   Treat chronic cough or constipation

-   Follow exercise timeline strictly

-   Use proper lifting techniques

## When to Contact Your Doctor Urgently

-   Severe or increasing pain

-   Fever

-   Redness or discharge from wound

-   Vomiting or abdominal swelling

-   Inability to pass urine or stool

## Final Reassurance

Most patients recover **smoothly and safely** after laparoscopic hernia surgery. Knowing what to expect helps you heal faster and with confidence.
